01What it is EVERYONE

Rock Solid Analytics turns what happens on the pitch into clear, shareable insight.

It does four things: you tag a match (live on the sideline, or later from video), it builds dashboards that show how your team is performing, it puts a live scoreboard online for supporters, and it lets you compare yourself anonymously against other clubs of your grade.

It runs in any phone or laptop browser — there's nothing to install. For quick access, open it once and use your browser's "Add to Home screen" so it sits like an app.

02Getting set up EVERYONE

The first time you sign in, you'll set your club up once:

  1. Choose your sport (football, hurling, camogie, ladies football).
  2. Pick your county, then find your club in the list.
  3. Set your colours — these drive the scoreboard and pitch markers.
  4. Choose your grade: Senior, Intermediate or Junior.
Grade matters. The anonymous cross-club ranking (the vs Others tab) only compares you against clubs of the same sport and grade. Getting this right means you're measured against the correct field.

04Before a match ANALYST

Open Edit match information to manage everything around a game — not just line-ups. For each match you can set the opponent, competition and round, the referee, the venue, whether it's Home / Away / Neutral, the weather, and assign players to positions for the squad.

These details aren't admin for its own sake — they power the Conditions dashboard (your record by referee, venue and weather), so the more you fill in, the richer that analysis becomes.

Tip. Keep opponent names spelled consistently. Several screens pool repeat fixtures by name — "Tinahely" tagged two different ways would show as two teams instead of one combined record.

05Importing existing data ANALYST

If you already tag in Performa, you can bring your history straight in.

  1. Export the match as a CSV from Performa.
  2. On the home screen choose Import data and select the file.
  3. The first time a new kind of label appears, you'll be asked to match it up once (e.g. which event a label means). Your choices are remembered for next time.
  4. Check the preview, then save — the match drops into your dashboards.
Other formats. Cleanly structured exports import smoothly. Very different or summary-only spreadsheets may not convert to match-by-match detail — when in doubt, send a sample through and check the preview before saving.

06Tagging a match live ANALYST

This is the heart of it — capturing the game from the sideline as it happens.

  1. Start a New match and confirm the basics (opponent, competition).
  2. Hold the phone in landscape. The match clock runs on its own.
  3. To log something, tap the event (shot, kickout, free, turnover, tackle…), tap where it happened on the pitch, choose the outcome, then the player number.
  4. Use Stop clock for stoppages, and View stats any time to see the game so far.
  5. When the game ends, finish the match — the final score is worked out from everything you tagged.
No signal? No problem. Every tag is saved to your phone the instant you make it, and syncs up automatically when signal returns. A pitch with no coverage won't lose you a single event — the indicator shows what's saved on the device and what's still waiting to sync.

07Tagging from video ANALYST

Couldn't tag live, or want to add detail? Tag the game back from footage.

  1. Open the Video tagger and load a video — a file from the phone, or a Dropbox link.
  2. Set the two sync points: mark the throw-in (0:00) and the start of the second half (30:00). The app then knows the game time of every moment.
  3. Tag exactly as you would live: event → location → outcome → player.
  4. Finish — the score recalculates from all the tags.
Keep it on the device. When you finish, you'll be asked whether to keep the video stored in the app (up to two at a time). Say yes and it's there for clips at training — no re-loading, works offline.

08Video clips at training ANALYST COACH

Any match kept on the device becomes a clip library. Open it from the "On this device" list in the Video tagger.

  • Filter the tagged events by team, event, outcome or player — "show me all his shots", "every turnover we conceded".
  • Tap a moment and the video jumps straight to it with a short lead-in, so you can show it instantly.
Two things to know. Clips only work for matches tagged from video (live-only games have no footage attached), and the video lives on the device that tagged it — the tags sync everywhere, the footage doesn't.

09Custom events ANALYST

Beyond the standard events, you can track things specific to how your club thinks about the game. Open Tagging template to add your own — give each one a name, decide whether it needs a pitch location, and add outcomes (which can carry a score if relevant).

Set a club default that appears in every game, and tweak it per match when you need something one-off. Your custom events show as a second row of buttons in the tagger and feed the scoreboard if they score — but they stay out of cross-club benchmarking, so comparisons remain fair.

10The live scoreboard EVERYONE

The Live mirror is a clean, big-screen scoreboard of the game in progress — score, scorers and the latest events — refreshing itself every few seconds. Cast it to a screen at the clubhouse, or share it with supporters.

Sharing it with supporters

  1. Open Live mirror and tap Share to grab your club's public link.
  2. Anyone who opens that link sees the live scoreboard — no login, no app.
  3. The link is permanent: pin it in the club WhatsApp once and it works for every game.
How it flows on match day. Share the link once, ever. Then just set up the new game and start tagging — the link automatically shows whichever match is currently live, populating in real time as you tag.
One live game at a time. A match shows as live until you finish it. Finish each game before starting the next and the link always points at the right one.

11Your dashboards EVERYONE

Open Stats to see your analysis, organised into tabs:

All League

The season overview — record, scoring, shot and kickout maps, player stats, and the headline trends across all your games.

Match Reports

The detail on any single game — shot maps, kickouts, timelines and the story of how it unfolded.

Benchmark

How each team you've played compares — kickout win %, shot efficiency, turnovers, tackles and 2-point conversion — with you highlighted. Repeat fixtures are pooled into one combined record.

Conditions

Your record and key metrics broken down by Referee, Venue (home/away/neutral) and Weather (wet vs dry) — the context around results.

vs Others

The one nobody else has. An anonymous ranking against every club of your sport and grade using the app — core metrics, each line (backs, midfield, forwards, bench) and the goalkeeper — showing your rank and the field average. You only ever see your own position, never another club's figures.

For coaches. You don't need to tag anything to use these — once your analyst has the data in, the dashboards, the live scoreboard and the tactical pad are all yours to read.

12Tactical pad COACH

A blank pitch with a full set of 1–15 jerseys for each team that you can drag around to explain a shape, a press, a kickout setup — whatever you need to show. Toggle a team off to focus on one, and reset to the starting formation any time. Quick, visual, and right there on your phone.

13GAA conventions ANALYST

So everyone tags consistently, the app follows standard GAA conventions:

Score notationGoals‑Points (e.g. 1‑08), frees shown in brackets
Scoring valuesGoal = 3 · 2‑Pointer = 2 · Point = 1
2‑point zoneBeyond the 40m arc (sideline to sideline)
KickoutsTaken from the 21m line
Second-half clockContinues from 30:00

The pitch you tap to place an event is the same premium pitch used everywhere in the app, so a shot you place lands in the same spot on the report map.

14Tips & fixes EVERYONE

  • Not seeing a new feature? Your browser may be showing a cached version — pull down to refresh, or add ?v= with a new number to the address.
  • Live tagging offline is fully supported — tags save to the phone and sync when signal returns. Wait for the "synced" state before closing if you can.
  • Video clips are per-device — the footage stays on the phone that tagged it; tags and stats sync everywhere.
  • Keep opponent and venue names consistent so repeat fixtures and grounds pool correctly.
  • Finish a game when it's over — that locks the result and clears it from the live scoreboard, keeping the public link pointed at the current match.
  • vs Others looks empty? It fills in as more clubs of your grade join — until then you'll see yourself at the top of a field of one.
